Creator

Domormu

Domormu is the sole developer behind Imperium Mortis.

Like many aspiring game developers, it started in the Warcraft III World Editor. After countless hours making maps that were never released and existed purely for personal enjoyment, one thought stayed: the game he wanted most did not exist.

Growing up with Warcraft III, World of Warcraft, Age of Empires, Old School RuneScape, Mount & Blade: Warband, and other games that made worlds feel worth living in, Domormu kept returning to the same idea. If nobody was making that game, he would try to make it himself.

Imperium Mortis has been shaped through more than seven years of off and on work, with the first groundwork beginning in 2016 after more than a decade of ideation. Parts of that work continued while Domormu was deployed to Qatar as Active Duty Air Force. He is now an Air Force veteran.

With a background in psychology and a lifelong fascination with strategy, roleplaying, worldbuilding, and horror, the goal was never to create another power fantasy where the player is destined to save the world. Imperium Mortis asks a different question: what happens when the tension of horror is combined with the consequence of strategy?

Can players care about a character with the same weight they care about the lives they build in The Sims? Can a lost limb, a dead companion, a starving settlement, or a failed expedition become memorable stories rather than temporary setbacks?

Every system, every mechanic, and every decision serves the same goal: creating a world where survival matters, leadership has consequences, and the stories worth remembering are the ones players create for themselves.
